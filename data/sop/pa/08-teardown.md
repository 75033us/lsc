# 08 · Teardown & Return-to-Office

**Purpose**: Power down without speaker pops, coil cables so they last another year, return everything to the office, lock up. Done in 20–30 minutes by one person who isn't rushing.
**Audience**: PA volunteer on duty.
**Last reviewed**: 2026-05-08 — Shine Zhang

> **Print this. Volunteers tend to rush teardown after a long Sunday — the printed checklist resists shortcuts.**

---

## Don't rush teardown

The tear-down phase is when most gear gets damaged at small churches. People are tired, kids are running around, someone's offering to help by "just unplugging things." **Slow down.** A cable mis-coiled in 30 seconds will cost a $20 cable in 6 months. A speaker popped in haste will require an amp repair.

If you're tired, take 5 minutes to talk to people first, then come back fresh.

---

## Order matters: power-down sequence

> **Reverse of power-on. Amp/speakers first, source last. Otherwise: speaker pops.**

- [ ] **Speakers / amp OFF first**
- [ ] Wireless mic receivers OFF (and remove batteries — mic switches are easy to bump in storage)
- [ ] Laptop: stop DJI recording (if still running) → save any open files → close PowerPoint → close Google Drive sync → shut down
- [ ] DJI camera OFF (after copying footage — see `07-recording.md`)
- [ ] Mixer OFF **last**
- [ ] Unplug power cables (mixer, laptop, amps)

Listen for: any pop at any step. If you hear one, you sequenced something wrong — note for next time.

---

## Disconnect signal cables (mixer-side first, source-side last)

> **Disconnect at the mixer first**, then walk to the source end. Avoids leaving live cables connected to live mics that could swing into things.

- [ ] Pull XLRs from mixer (gently, by the connector body — never yank the cable)
- [ ] Pull mic cables from mics
- [ ] Pull HDMI from laptop and projector
- [ ] Pull stage monitor cable from monitor

---

## Coil cables (the part everyone gets wrong)

> **Over-under. Always.**

The over-under technique:
1. Loop 1: standard loop (over)
2. Loop 2: reverse the natural twist (under)
3. Loop 3: standard (over)
4. Continue alternating

Why: cables have an internal twist memory. Always-same-direction wrapping fights that memory and breaks internal wires over time. Over-under preserves the memory and the cable lasts years instead of months.

**Don't**:

- Wrap around your elbow / forearm. Ever.
- Yank a cable to "shake out" tangles.
- Coil tightly — keep loops at least 12" diameter.
- Pinch the cable in a tight bend at the connector. Strain relief matters.

After coiling, secure with the cable's hook-and-loop tie (every cable should have one — if not, add Velcro). Avoid plastic zip ties (can cut into jacket on long-term storage).

---

## Pack up

- [ ] Cables in cable bag, organized by type (XLRs together, power separately, etc.)
- [ ] Mics in mic bag/case (foam pouch each — no loose mics)
- [ ] Mixer: place rear-side-down on the carry surface (don't flip onto knobs)
- [ ] Laptop in laptop bag
- [ ] DJI in DJI case, on charging cradle in office
- [ ] Booth kit closed — verify contents per `01-equipment-inventory.md` § "Booth kit"

**Inventory the booth kit** — anything missing or low (battery count, USB sticks)? Note for restocking.

---

## Return to office

- [ ] Two trips, not one (don't stack)
- [ ] Trip 1: laptop + booth kit + mics + DJI
- [ ] Trip 2: mixer + cable bag
- [ ] Place gear in its assigned office storage spot — same place every week so retrieval is fast
- [ ] **Lock the office.** Verify the door is locked, not just closed.

---

## Sign-off

- [ ] Send a quick message in the team chat: "Teardown complete. <Anything notable>." (E.g., "ch 7 mic battery dying — replaced, but get a new one for next week.")
- [ ] If anything is broken or missing: tag Shine immediately, don't wait until next Sunday.

## Cable-care quick reference (for the printed booth card)

```
✗ Wrap around elbow            ✓ Over-under coil
✗ Yank by the cable            ✓ Pull by the connector
✗ Step on cables               ✓ Tape walkway crossings
✗ Tight 90° bends              ✓ Strain relief at connectors
✗ Plastic zip ties             ✓ Hook-and-loop ties
```
