# 04 · Service Runsheet (cue-by-cue)

**Purpose**: What you do, in order, from T-0 to "service over." Mostly listening, watching, and small adjustments — but the small adjustments make or break the experience.
**Audience**: PA volunteer on duty.
**Last reviewed**: 2026-05-08 — Shine Zhang

---

## The shape of a Sunday

Typical flow (adjust to actual order of service):

1. Pre-service music
2. Welcome / call to worship
3. Worship set (3–4 songs)
4. Announcements
5. Offering
6. Sermon
7. Response / closing song
8. Benediction
9. Post-service music

Sunday school content (if any) lives in the same room — treat it as another "ministry segment" within the runsheet.

## Slide-clicking discipline (sermon segment specifically)

The clicker is your most active control during the sermon. Common mistakes:

- **Clicking too early** — pastor lingers on a slide longer than the deck implies. Wait for verbal/visual cue.
- **Missing a click** — pastor moves on but the slide doesn't. Click promptly when noticed.
- **Backtracking visibly** — congregation sees the deck rewind. **Don't.** If you over-clicked, just stay on the wrong slide until the next natural transition.
- **Clicker dies** — Norwii N29 has a small LED. If it stops responding, walk to the laptop and use the keyboard arrow keys. (The runsheet card has this written on it for booth reference.)

---

## Watching levels

Throughout the service, scan:

1. **Master meter** — peaks should land yellow, occasionally orange, **never red** for more than a flash.
2. **PFL any channel that sounds wrong** — if the pastor sounds quiet, PFL Ch 11/12 to verify input level vs. fader position.
3. **AUX 1 (monitor)** — if a worship-team member is gesturing at their monitor mid-song, dial up the AUX 1 of the source they need.

---

## Mic discipline

- **One person on stage = one mic open at a time**, ideally. Multiple open mics = phase issues, possible feedback.
- **Pastor mic during worship**: usually stays open at unity (he might come up between songs to lead).
- **Pastor mic during offering**: open
- **Pastor mic during music interludes between songs**: open if he's speaking, otherwise pull fader down a touch (don't mute — easier to recover)

---

## Common in-service interventions

| Situation | Action |
| --- | --- |
| Pastor's mic suddenly silent | Check wireless receiver LED; battery may have died → run a backup mic to the pulpit |
| Worship vocal feeding back | Pull AUX 1 send on that channel down, not the FOH fader (the issue is monitor → mic distance) |
| Slide didn't advance with clicker | Walk to laptop, advance via arrow key; replace clicker battery at first natural break |
| Music continues after pastor starts speaking | Quickly pull Ch 15/16 fader down; check that pre-service playlist isn't on auto-loop |
| DJI camera stopped recording | Note it; restart at next natural break; do **not** disrupt the service to fix |

For the full incident library, see `09-incident-playbook.md`.
