# 10 · Volunteer Onboarding

**Purpose**: How a brand-new volunteer goes from "I don't know what a fader is" to "I solo'd a Sunday and it was fine." Designed as **on-demand, 1-on-1** progression — no cohort to wait for.
**Audience**: New volunteers + their trainer.
**Last reviewed**: 2026-05-08 — Shine Zhang

---

## The progression

```
Read SOP → Class 1 → Class 2 → Class 3 → Class 4 → Class 5 → Class 6 → Solo sign-off
   ↓          ↓         ↓         ↓         ↓         ↓         ↓
 1-2 hrs    Watch    Hands    Hands    Hands    Run a    Solo + sign-off
            video     -on     -on at   -on     recorded
                     gear &   the      slides   service
                     cables   mixer    workflow
```

**Pace**: as fast as the trainee absorbs. Some people will go from start to solo in 4 weeks. Others will take 4 months. There's no shame in either.

## Class milestones

Each "class" is a ~45-minute session covering the topic, then hands-on practice. See `dev/task_013_pa_sop.md` for the curriculum overview.

| # | Class topic | Pre-read | Hands-on milestone | Ready to advance when… |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

| 2 | Gear & cables — protect what we have | `02-pre-service.md`, `08-teardown.md` | Coil 5 cables over-under correctly; identify XLR vs. TRS vs. speaker; demo the power-on / power-off sequence | Trainee can coil any cable correctly, unprompted. |
| 3 | MG16XU hands-on | `01-equipment-inventory.md`, `03-rehearsal.md`, `training/sean-mixer-setup/notes.md` | Set TRIM on 4 mics via PFL; build a basic worship mix from rehearsal audio | Trainee can find PFL, TRIM, AUX 1 PRE, EQ controls without looking. |
| 4 | Slides workflow | `05-slides-and-laptop.md` | Receive a USB → virus check → copy → merge with worship deck → run with clicker | Trainee can run the full handoff workflow without prompting. |

## Sign-off rubric

A trainee is ready to solo when:

- [ ] **All ten SOP files read**, with questions resolved
- [ ] **Sean's video** watched (`training/sean-mixer-setup/notes.md`)
- [ ] **Class 6 solo** completed without trainer intervention
- [ ] Trainee can answer these questions cold:
  - What does PRE on AUX 1 do, and why is it always pressed?
  - What's the power-on sequence and why?
  - What do you do if the pastor's mic goes silent?
  - What's the T-15 rule?
  - Where do you click if the clicker dies?
- [ ] Trainee has the office key (or knows where it lives) and the PA-account password

Sign-off: trainer (typically Shine) records the date in the volunteer log, and the new volunteer goes into the rotation.

---

## Buddy system

For the **first 4 services solo**, a buddy is in the room — not at the booth, just present and reachable. Buddy stays on call if anything escalates.

After 4 successful solos, the new volunteer is a full rotation member.

---

## Trainer responsibilities

When you're the trainer:

- **Don't take the controls.** Even when you watch them about to make a mistake. Talk them through it. They learn faster from one near-miss than from ten of you doing it for them.
- **Document each session** — what was covered, what the trainee struggled with, what to revisit
- **Be honest at sign-off**. Don't pass someone who isn't ready. The cost is one un-fun Sunday for the trainee; the cost of letting them solo too soon is breaking gear or breaking the service.

---

## Re-onboarding (returning volunteers)

If a volunteer has been off the rotation for 6+ months:

- Re-read `02-pre-service.md`, `04-service-runsheet.md`, `09-incident-playbook.md`
- Shadow one Sunday before solo
- Skip the formal classes unless they want a refresher
